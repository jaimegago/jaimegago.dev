I started on the systems that scale when a billion people ask a phone to do
something. Siri made me fluent in the particular ergonomics of latency,
fan-out, and the weight of a misheard syllable at scale — and it gave me the
habits I still reach for when something is on fire in production.

From there, a long detour through Stanford Medicine: the problem space where
correctness is measured in people, not percentiles, and where the most
interesting engineering question is usually *"which uncertainty are you
willing to own?"* Most recently I have been leading platform engineering at
LGT, which is where the current crop of thoughts on developer tooling came
from.

I am self-taught, and the interesting thesis I have been chasing is that
AI is mostly a discovery story rather than an invention one: the structures
that already exist in human engineering practice are getting uncovered, not
invented. That is the lens I am writing and building from for the next stretch.
